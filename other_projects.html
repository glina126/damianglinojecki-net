Bluetooth RGB LED Strip



I always wanted to have a LED strip above my desk for when i work on my projects or do school work. I tend to do most of my projects on paper first or on a white board, non the less, whenever you write on paper you get shadows, this was annoying. So, in order to eliminate the shadows I came up with the idea of a LED strip above my desk. Just a simple white LED strip was too simple, so I decided to go with a white strip and an RGB LED strip. Why both? more light and more of a pure white light. It is hard to mix the RGB leds to a good white color and besides, one strip is too dim. The combination of the two makes it a perfect work and study environment.

pic of the hardware

The circuit and code is simple. I have an Arduino pro mini soldered onto a custom home etched PCB, a few transistors on the PCB itself and a breakout connection for a bluetooth module. This enables me to control any of the colors precisely and wirelessly form practically any device.

The code for this project was simple. the Arduino checks the bluetooth buffer and processes it whenever there is something available. The processing application then connects to the HC-05 bluetooth module via serial communication and then sends the WRGB values over on color change. This mechanism works without flaws. I kept the baund rate down to 9600bps thus the refresh rate is slow, but sufficient for this purpose. After all this was a quick and dirty project.
